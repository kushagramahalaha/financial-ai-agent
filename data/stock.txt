[STOCK_MARKET_BASICS]

Stocks represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a portion of that company.

Types of Stocks:
- Large Cap: Stable companies (e.g., Reliance, TCS)
- Mid Cap: Growth potential, moderate risk
- Small Cap: High risk, high return

Key Concepts:
- Market Capitalization = Share Price × Total Shares
- Dividend: Profit shared with investors
- Capital Gain: Profit from selling stock

---

[VALUATION_METRICS]

1. P/E Ratio (Price to Earnings)
Formula: P/E = Market Price / EPS
- High P/E → Growth expectation
- Low P/E → Undervalued or risky

2. EPS (Earnings Per Share)
Formula: EPS = Net Profit / Total Shares

3. ROE (Return on Equity)
Formula: ROE = Net Income / Shareholder Equity

---

[TRADING_TYPES]

1. Intraday Trading:
- Buy and sell within same day
- High risk

2. Swing Trading:
- Hold for days/weeks

3. Long-Term Investing:
- Hold for years

---

[RISK_FACTORS]

- Market Risk (economic changes)
- Company Risk (bad management)
- Liquidity Risk
