[MUTUAL_FUNDS_BASICS]

A mutual fund pools money from investors and invests in stocks, bonds, or other assets.

Managed by: Fund Manager

---

[TYPES_OF_FUNDS]

1. Equity Funds:
- Invest in stocks
- High risk, high return

2. Debt Funds:
- Invest in bonds
- Low risk

3. Hybrid Funds:
- Mix of equity + debt

---

[POPULAR_CATEGORIES]

- Large Cap Fund
- Mid Cap Fund
- Index Fund
- ELSS (Tax Saving)
- Sectoral Fund

---

[SIP_VS_LUMPSUM]

1. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan):
- Invest fixed amount regularly
- Reduces market timing risk

2. Lumpsum:
- One-time investment
- Best in falling markets

---

[KEY_METRICS]

1. NAV (Net Asset Value):
- Price of fund unit

2. Expense Ratio:
- Fund management fee

3. AUM (Assets Under Management):
- Total fund size

---

[RISK_LEVELS]

- Low: Debt Funds
- Medium: Hybrid Funds
- High: Equity Funds

---

[TAXATION]

- Equity Funds:
  - STCG: 15%
  - LTCG: 10%

- Debt Funds:
  - Taxed as per income slab

---

[EXAMPLE]

If you invest ₹5000/month via SIP for 12 months:
Total Invested = ₹60,000
Returns depend on market performance
